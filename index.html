Growth is the underlying passion that drives nearly everything I do. Growth comes in many forms; finding and solving problems, building awareness, and finding ways to improve are just a few. For the last eight years, I've focused on growth by researching, exploring data, and finding insights that make things better.

I don't subscribe to the philosophy "if it ain't broke, don't fix it." My philosophy is, "expect it to break but continuously look for ways to prevent it."

I have embraced this philosophy by focusing on constant improvement using the following process: (1) know where you are, (2) see where you want to be, and (3) plan how to get there. I live out this philosophy best through research and analysis, visualizations and presentations, reports, and personal development.

As a business and data analyst, I achieve this philosophy using a variety of tools. Whether using Microsoft PowerPoint or Tableau to present the information, Python, SQL, or Microsoft Excel to gather insights or research, my aim is always at some form of improvement.

Let's connect if your organization is looking to use data for growth or want to chat.
